Tryouts

Tryouts Checklist

  1. Fill out the Interest Form - we'll be sending updates, tryouts prep session dates, resources, and more via email. Join our Schoology group too - the code is WK53-XPN3-TJ2PW.
  2. Choose your events. Everyone has different strengths and likes, so take some time and read the descriptions and the linked scioly.org pages for the events above. Once you have an idea of which events you'd like to do, indicate what they are in the Tryouts Signup form.
  3. If you want to take a shot for the States team, feel free to submit a States Team Application by Thursday, September 14th. There are some essay questions so please give yourself enough time to formulate thoughtful responses. All grade levels and experience levels are welcome to apply!
  4. Study and practice for your events! Using the scioly.org pages above, the scioly.org test archive, the Rulebook, and anything else you can find, prepare for the tests you will be taking.
  5. Go to tryouts! Be confident, get a good night's sleep, and best of luck!

The States Team

Woodson Science Olympiad consists of three teams: one States team and two Regional teams. The Regional teams exhibit similar levels of performance and participate in most Invitationals and the Regional tournament. However, they do not proceed to the States tournament. On the other hand, the States team is comprised of the top-performing individuals. Members of the States team have the unique opportunity attend tournaments such as the Princeton Invitational and the Virginia States tournament, in addition to other competitions the Regionals teams participate in.

In the past, our team structure was relatively rigid, which meant that skilled individuals on the Regional teams did not have the chance to compete at Princeton or States. To address this, we've made some important changes. With the exception of the three members of the Executive Board, every member of the States team can be substituted for the entire season. This means that high-performing members from the Regional teams have the opportunity to move up to the States team if they earn better rankings.

Currently, we have five vacant spots on our States team. Everyone is welcome to apply, and you can find more information regarding the applation process in the Tryouts Guide.
