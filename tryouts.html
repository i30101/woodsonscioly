Tryouts

How to Get In

The tryouts process to get into Woodson Science Olympiad may seem daunting, but worry not! Just review the steps below.

  1. Decide which events you're going to do. You'll be able to see events and event pairings in the Tryouts Guide, which we'll be releasing soon.
  2. Sign up for those events. We'll be posting a form soon where you can indicate which events you want to try out for.
  3. Feeling competitive? Apply for the States team! Learn about the benefits and expectations for those on the States team and the application process.
  4. Prepare for your events. Go to the "Resources" section to see how you can prepare for those events. We'll be posting the rulebook and an in-depth tryouts guide after September 5th.
  5. Go to tryouts! Stay calm and best of luck. You've got this!

The States Team

Woodson Science Olympiad consists of three teams: one States team and two Regional teams. The Regional teams exhibit similar levels of performance and participate in most Invitationals and the Regional tournament. However, they do not proceed to the States tournament. On the other hand, the States team is comprised of the top-performing individuals. Members of the States team have the unique opportunity attend tournaments such as the Princeton Invitational and the Virginia States tournament, in addition to other competitions the Regionals teams participate in.

In the past, our team structure was relatively rigid, which meant that skilled individuals on the Regional teams did not have the chance to compete at Princeton or States. To address this, we've made some important changes. With the exception of the three members of the Executive Board, every member of the States team can be substituted for the entire season. This means that high-performing members from the Regional teams have the opportunity to move up to the States team if they earn better rankings.

Currently, we have five vacant spots on our States team. Everyone is welcome to apply, and we will soon release an application form along with more detailed instructions in our Tryouts Guide.
